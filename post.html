Macros: The Building Blocks for Nutrition

There are three types of macronutrients: carbohydrates, fats, and proteins. We will discuss each on a basic surface level so feel free to do further research.

Carbohydrates are the main source of energy our bodies use. Our bodies turn these carbs into glucose which is what our cells use for energy. Good sources of carbs include pasta, bread, potatoes, rice, and legumes.

Fats are another source of energy our bodies can use and they are more dense than carbs. Healthy fats are important for your body and brain to work properly. Examples include fish oil, dairy, avocados, olive oil, nuts, and most redmeats.

Proteins are very important because all of your cells and their sturcture are made from protein. Our bodies make a lot of the protein we need but it also needs additional outside sources. Good sources include meats, greek yogurt, and nuts.
